degvelsku 1.0

Description

Degvelsku is a transcription using fingers of roman alphabet, and adapted to french, this natural language being the object of degvelsku's first use.

The general idea is to use the pressure or not (of the fingers (of a hand of the speaker)) on a harm, thigh or shoulder of the addresse to convey informations through a combinations of fingers ⦅their pressure⦆.

The speaker puts one of its hands over one part of the body of the addresse. The addresse can do the same on the speaker, and so have a short-latency conversation.

The idea described by the present paper was brought to reality and shaped by Louis Escande. It is distributed under CC-BY-4.0. Feel free to send feedback or question to melopee7 (at) gmail (dot) com.

Understanding romanization
  • 0 : no finger ⦅and ␣ is space⦆
  • 125 : fingers 1, 2 and 5 are pressed
  • -3 : fingers 1, 2, 4, and 5 are pressed
  • numbers in bold font describe fingers combinations; the cell next to a bold number is the sigil produced
More symbols
  • ( ) : ,ot only used for parenthesis. In fact, they are 'startor' and 'terminator' of an expression. To precise this expression, some conventional coding are used.
    • s( ... )s : mathematical/separation parenthesis
    • x( ... )x : mathematical expression ⦅see below for additional rules⦆
    • q( ... )q : quotation
    • h( ... )h : element
    • c( ... )c : commentary
    • k( ... )k : editorial insertion
  • \ : general accent purpose. As for ( ), some code can stated, but for simplicity's sake, \ can be used for any accents if the speaker or addresse think want, even they are thinking about a peculiar accent ⦅this practise works well in french, where some accents are too peculiar or redundant, e.g. c̨, ë, ù, à, ê⦆. Might be also used for defining new symbols, e.g. @
  • , and . : to generate more punctuations characters, some repetitions of the fingers pattern for . or , are made.
    • .. : :
    • ,. : ;
    • ,, : ?
    • ., : !
  • Numbers are made with some special strings of consonants, which, I hope, not existing as words in the language wanted to use with degvelsku. If ambiguity, maybe add \ before the number string. Digit are, as in positional notation, concatenate.

Nota Bene: due to short space for various symbols and need of enough speed, some symbols can be dropped if the speaker or addresse want ⦅if they are understanding each other it seems to be good⦆, e.g. the terminator semantic character, e.g. 's' in ')s' is not ambiguous to drop.

Mnemonics and building's relations
  • 1 finger pressed : vowels
  • 2 fingers pressed : fricatives
  • 3 fingers pressed : occlusives
  • 4 fingers pressed : punctuations
  • 5 fingers pressed : y ⦅not oftenly used in french⦆
