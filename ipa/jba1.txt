        ⁕ why {traji} between {dizlo} and {tonga}?
        it's not the tone which is extreme
          ※ In Lujvo and Tanru, modifiers like mutce/traji/zenba etc are put   before the property they modify, e.g. {bratce} = mutce loka barda;   {braze'a} = zenba loka barda... When in the lujvo/tanru expansion one  of  the elements goes in an abstraction that is given as an argument to  the  other lujvo/tanru element, that second element is considered the  main  one (the lujvo/tanru head), and therefore comes last, because  tanru and  lujvo are head-final (modifiers comes after modified  elements). In  {sutra zebna}, "zenba" is the head/modifier, and sutra is  the modified  element.
        aren't tanru left-associative?
        if yes, {mutce loka barda} will be (((mutce) loka) barda)
        and that's work
          ※ xaskoiklabrebi'ozu'e → [([(xamsi korbi) klama] bredi) binxo] zukte;   tanru elements are grouped from the left. But the main element, which   gives it's argument structure to the whole bridi, is the last one  (here,  zukte), in the case of tanru at least.
          The orders of tanru and lujvo is unlike that of the other parts of   Lojban, which is mostly head-initial, with the outstanding exception of   lujvo and tanru. (In French, the equivalents of tanru are head  initial,  for example "papillon de nuit" vs {nicte toldi} in Lojban).
